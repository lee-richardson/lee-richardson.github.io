Building trust in an e-commerce brand

What was the project?

Creating a feature that explains pricing to new users, and builds trust in using the product.

My role

  • — Creation of user survey.
  • — Affinity mapping.
  • — Solution concepts.
  • — Wireframes.

Description

I created a survey to find what users thought about e-commerce brands, and introduced a feature of transparent pricing. I worked as the lead designer on the project, and the task took one week including user testing.

The problem

As an e-commerce startup, we needed to stand out in the competitive market and build trust with users.

The outcome

A feature that explained product pricing that led to less cart abandonment and more checkout conversions.

Research

A significant part of achieving success in e-commerce is establishing trust. While we already had customers, business requirements were to enhance trust in our brand, which would lead to improved conversion rates and higher customer retention.


To kickstart the project, I initiated a whiteboard exercise with Wantboard's team. This session allowed us to brainstorm ideas related to trust. It also set the stage for benchmarking, helping us define how we could distinguish ourselves from competitors.


In order to gain insights into our users' preferences and gather qualitative data, I designed a survey focusing on online shopping habits, user expectations, and opinions related to Wantboard and other e-commerce brands. This survey was distributed through our email newsletter, with 40% of our users completing it.


I analysed the collected data using affinity mapping, which revealed patterns of what to focus on in terms of new features. Our findings highlighted that users desired more information and clarity throughout the shopping process. I carried out competitor analysis to identify any common patterns or features that emerged among successful e-commerce products.


I found an interesting user interface element used by Everlane, an ethical clothing company. Focused on transparency, their website breaks down the cost of a product through an infographic. This aligned with the user needs found in my affinity mapping results, so I decided to follow the idea of introducing this as a feature.

Design

Since our model had cost components such as sourcing, shipping, and other associated fees, they were added on top of the original product price. I aimed to make the pricing more engaging for customers, understanding that it shouldn't be a dull list of numbers.


I started sketching ideas on how to represent each fee hierarchically. Eventually, I condensed this concept into an 'umbrella' that encompassed all additional fees, offering room for small illustrations.

A wireframe of the feature.

The goal was to ensure that users had a comprehensive understanding of what they were buying, avoiding any surprises or unexpected costs. This approach provided users with a final price before reaching the checkout stage, resulting in transparency and user confidence.

Different visual treatments of the feature were explored. This uses the same red shade as the brand's logo.

Prototype

I moved from initial sketches to creating a low-fidelity prototypes exploring various layouts and visual styles, especially for the mobile site. To enhance the visual appeal of the feature, I incorporated icons that aimed to give a sense of lightheartedness to the topic of money.


After a prototype was created, I organised a user testing session over one afternoon. During this session, I observed the interactions of five users to learn their thoughts around the concept of transparent pricing.

Build

After receiving positive feedback, the prototype was refined into the final design, and the feature was handed over to developers for implementation. Three months later, we conducted another user survey through an email newsletter. We collected qualitative data and compared it an earlier survey, which revealed that the feature had increased user trust.

The transparent pricing feature allows users to see the full cost of an item before checkout, with no surprises.

Successes

Data analytics showed that after users interacting with the feature, checkout conversions had increased by 40% after two months.
