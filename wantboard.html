Building trust in an e-commerce brand

What was the project?

I created a new feature that made the browsing flow easier and drove up user conversions.

Company

Wantboard

Timeline

Jan 2018 - Sep 2018

Methods

Wireframing, prototype testing, affinity mapping, competitive analysis, user surveys, UI elements.

Description

I used a survey to find what users thought about e-commerce brands, and introduced a feature of transparent pricing. I worked as the lead designer on the project, and the task took three days including user testing.

The challenge

As an e-commerce startup, we needed to stand out in the competitive market and build trust with users.

The goal

Introduce a feature that builds trust in our brand among customers.

Discover

A large part of succeeding in e-commerce is trust. We had customers but wanted to build trust in the brand to improve our conversion rate and customer retention.

To kickoff the feature project, I started the project by facilitating a whiteboard exercise session with Wantboard’s staff. This gave us time to quickly ideate and brainstorm ideas around trust. This also set up our benchmarking in how we wanted to stand out against competitors.

To understand our users and collect qualitative data, I created a survey focusing on online shopping habits, and user’s expectations and opinions around Wantboard and other e-commerce brands. This survey was sent through an email newsletter and around 40% of users completed the survey. I analyzed the data using affinity mapping to identify patterns and generate new ideas. The grouping of data showed that our users would prefer more information and clarity during the shopping process.

To get a better understanding of customer trust, I performed a competitor analysis session. This allowed me to focus on e-commerce user flows and see if any common patterns or features emerged.

Define

Using sketching and rapid prototyping, I iterated on providing clear and concise product information throughout the user journey, from browsing to checkout. This would ensure that users are well-informed about what they are buying, with no surprises or unexpected costs.

After iteration, I decided to introduce a feature of ‘Transparent Pricing’ with a visual breakdown of a product’s cost. In the form of an infographic, different cost components such as manufacturing, shipping, and labour fees would be shown. This would result in a final price shown before the user gets to the checkout stage.

Develop

I moved on from sketches into low-fidelity prototypes, experimenting with layout and visual language. Iconography was added to bring more visual appeal to the feature. I facilitated a user testing session over one afternoon, viewing how five users interacted with a high-fidelity prototype and their response to the idea of transparent pricing.

Deliver

With feedback positive, I added a small detail of incorporating the U.S. and Canadian flags into the price tags to reflect cross-border prices and provide some contrast. The feature was handed off to developers and implemented.

Result

After three months, users were surveyed in another email newsletter. Around 45% of users completed the survey. Quantitative data was collected and compared to the earlier research survey. The post-implementation survey showed that by displaying the feature on product pages, user trust around shopping with Wantboard had increased by 60%.

The transparent pricing feature allows users to see the full cost of an item before checkout, with no surprises.
