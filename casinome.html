Overview

With a huge number of games, users needed some help narrowing down their options. Our products already had search and category sub-categorisation, but we added an extra feature - provider filtering. I worked with another designer on the project.

The challenge

Designing for a mobile-first audience, we need a flow that worked easily on a small screen. We also had to design for the Japanese market, which is used to having a lot of visual information on screen at once.

With our users being Japanese, and most game providers being European, we learned that our players could maybe not recognise names of game providers in English. We decided the best solution was to go use provider logos instead of text. We collected a list of all game providers and sourced their logos, using wireframes and Figma prototypes to ideate the flow of the filter.

In order not to overwhelm the user, we decided the filter must initially show only a couple of rows of options, and not the dozens that were available. Users would also be able to combine their filtering with a search term, which would have visual priority in the hierarchy. We tested this multiple times to make sure things weren’t too confusing.

Once the flow was optimised for ease-of-use, we applied the UI to the wireframes. Data showed that users found their favourite games faster as a result.
