Redesigned a digital product

DealTap

2018 - 2019

Lee

Methods ↴

Wireframing, prototype testing, personas, card sorting, competitive analysis, stakeholder interviews, user interviews and testing, UI elements.

Description ↴

For a major update to the app, give a redesign to flow and UI.

The challenge

Give a better experience to our users.

The goal

Improve functionality and the user experience compared to competitors, and to design easy-to-use interfaces. A product team consisting of a product owner, developers, and myself as designer was formed.

The process

Understand

To start things off, we did a lot of research. We looked at the timeline for the project, what other similar websites were doing, and looked at the technical side of things to see if there were any limitations we needed to be aware of. I organized a one-day workshop with stakeholders to kick off the project. We looked at what other websites like ours were doing and what information we had about how users use our website.

We talked specifically about the most important things our website does, like making new documents, filling out forms, and signing and sharing documents. We wanted to make sure we were doing these things well and see if there were any ways we could improve them.

Research

I looked at other similar websites to see what they were doing well and what we could learn from them. Looking at websites that weren't just related to real estate gave me new ideas about how to add signature functionality.

Research into other products aimed at real estate agents.

I conducted user testing to ensure it met the needs of real estate agents. During a week-long testing session, agents of different experience levels completed tasks. We then ran card-sorting sessions to gather user feedback on the app's structure, and conducted interviews to understand their daily work processes using DealTap.

Card-sorting sessions helped us understand user needs.

Analyze

I compared task completion times and identified pain points. The most common issues were related to signing documents, document history, and auto-save. These issues were classified as high impact, meaning they prevented users from completing other tasks. Other issues were categorized as moderate or low impact.

I used feedback from users to identify three personas and used them to guide our ideas. Testing showed that users needed faster access to main features, so we focused on improving offer generation, sign and share, and PDF markup.

Three personas were created to guide our decision making.

Sketch

After identifying the main plot points, I defined the primary paths the personas would take through the app. Creating several user journeys for each of our personas was the best way to structure content and functionality. Using storyboards, I generated ideas about the arrangement of UI, functional and data elements, and interactive behaviours.

User journey maps were created to understand different flows for different types of user.

Paper sketches helped flesh out ideas for flow optimization.

Wireframes and prototype

Wireframing helped interactions and the layout to come together. When we had a fleshed-out user journey, we ran usability testing over a one-week period for feedback on if the new changes met user needs.

Skeleton wireframes were produced and tested by power users.

Test design and iterate

Following user testing I began work on a new design system based on common UI patterns, with a focus on meeting web accessibility standards. The new UI aimed to eliminate any unnecessary elements, with a clear, readable typography and a high-contrast color theme. We prioritized readability, as real estate agents often work outdoors and in low-light conditions.

Some of the new components that were created.

Result

While work on the UI was ongoing, Dealtap was acquired by Remine, a U.S. real estate platform. This UX research was added to its plans around product optimization.
