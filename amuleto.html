Attracting new users with a unique UI

What was the project?

Creating a new brand that increases conversions with an easy-to-find registration process and a polished user interface.

My role

Brand strategy, UX research, UX design, UI design.

Description

For half a year, I collaborated closely with another designer to develop a new brand. Our focus was to create a unique user interface, create an inviting landing page, and streamline the signup process.

The problem

In a crowded market, we needed to stand out from competitors and entice new users to sign up and engage with the product.

The outcome

A new brand with a distinct Unique Selling Point (USP).

Discover

A product team was created to focus on the brand, working in agile sprints. A product owner was hired in Brazil, to give cultural context and insights. The rest of the team included:

  • • Stakeholders (business analyst, payments and risk)
  • • Developers
  • • Two designers - myself and another product designer, collaborating daily.

Some initial notes from the project kickoff meeting focusing on business requirements.

To launch in line with the team’s backlog, business requirements wanted to redesign another Hero product and brand it ‘AmuletoBet’. This would be a Minimum Viable Product (MVP), keeping the existing product’s user journey. This meant design work was to create a brand identity and redesign the homepage to drive new users into the signup flow. Our Key Performance Indicator (KPI) focused on a new user signing up and completing a first deposit into their digital wallet.

We were tasked with redesigning this existing Hero product.

Define

We had a competitor analysis session to learn more about the market and give us clear evidence for our design decisions. We chose 8 direct competitors and defined parameters, including:

  • • The user journey, focusing on the path to signup and first deposit.
  • • Design patterns.
  • • Visual UI design.
  • • Dark UX patterns.

I took screenshots during competitor analysis to track user flows, focusing on signup.

In terms of UI, we quickly noticed the competition was not localized to Brazil. Instead, sites designed in Asia, Europe and the US offered Brazilian players an identical homepage design with a language selector. This gave us an opportunity for an Unique-Selling-Point (USP) to design the homepage to cater to a Brazilian audience.

During competitor analysis we noticed products shared aesthetic features, which gave us an opportunity to differentiate our product.

In terms of UX, we found competitors required a new user to sign up to access features, and even to see which games were offered. For our user flow, we focused on:

  • • Highlighting our features and USPs on the homepage to boost user’s interest.
  • • Allow games to be played in a ‘free-to-play’ mode, to motivate users to sign up and play for real.
  • • Ease-of-use around the signup flow. Starting the flow should be no more than three clicks away from any screen.
  • • After signup or login, users are directed to the product’s sport betting features, and not casino games. This was to attract users into initially using the product.

A rough sketch of landing page ideation, with focus on USPs.

We presented our findings to the product team and used a 4-hour brainstorming workshop to iterate brand directions. We ended with four possible options.

We presented the brand directions to product management.

Early UI ideas for the brand directions helped show our design thinking.

We presented these in a product team meeting, with the casino chip idea being chosen to develop because of its link to casino games and the green-and-yellow design being an USP based on the Brazilian flag. I wanted to confirm the user flow was optimized to improve signup rates. This meant investigating the site’s structure. Over a day, I created a sitemap that gave a view of how the screens are prioritized, related and categorized.

A screenshot of the sitemap, focusing on the initial flow for a new user.

Develop

I created low-fidelity prototypes of the entire product. We guerilla tested with five employees of Hero Gaming outside of the AmuletoBet product team. The task-completion list was:

  • • Starting from the landing page, complete the signup flow.
  • • Make a first deposit into their digital wallet.

Overall results were positive with no issues raised.

Wireframes that we guerilla user tested to gather insights into the flow.

While working on the UI, we realized that the lucky casino chip design didn’t connect with the brand. Product management were now expecting the product’s sports features would be more popular than the casino games. When we tested the logo on sports-focused material, the design felt out of place. Also the design didn’t stand out when placed against competitors.

We created this brand direction, but decided to rework our focus because of a disconnect between the UI and sports imagery.

In a meeting with the product team, we decided to scrap this idea and focus on another previously defined idea around luxury. We began more competitor analysis and noticed aesthetics based on game assets and cartoon mascots. To stand out with an elegant and refined design, we researched brands connected with luxury. As our product owner said, “Imagine James Bond in Monaco instead of a weekend in Las Vegas.” This was now our USP.

Through iteration, we created a dark colour palette that signified luxury and high-end brands. In week-long ideation sessions, the logo developed into a combined ‘A’ and ‘B’ representing AmuletoBet. During guerilla user testing with five Hero employees, this was found to be more readable on the dark UI than the original concept.

Development of the logo, from top-left to bottom-right.

Wireframes of the landing page, which were iterated for layout ideas.

We tried different imagery to highlight the idea of giving users a luxury experience.

In terms of UX, I created wireframes of every screen, and cross-checked them against the site map to confirm the user journey made sense. In order to speed up the UI workflow, I built a design system using components designed by myself and my colleague. Based on the wireframes, I then spent a few days creating high-fidelity prototypes in Figma with the UI applied.

We built high-fidelity prototypes to confirm the user flow met our goals. I created a design system to speed up the process.

To solve the business requirement of getting users to the signup flow quickly, we contained the signup flow in a sliding drawer, which was always accessible from the sidebar menu when logged out. We guerilla tested the core user flow with 5 Hero Gaming employees who were unfamiliar with the product. Each test confirmed that the signup flow was easy to find and understandable, and was completed with no issues.

Completing the signup flow was a main KPI. Our high-fidelity prototype test confirmed that the signup flow was easily findable.

Deliver

Comfortable that the user flow met our product goals, the UI was checked. We focused on readability and meeting accessibility standards, before presenting to the product team, and implementing.

The delivered UI for the landing page header, emphasizing USPs and a different experience to our competitors. The product was launched in spring 2022.

Successes

A user interviews session carried out by an agency in Brazil showed overall positive user feedback including:

  • • Users were not confused or appeared lost during the main flow of signup and deposit.
  • • The aesthetic of high-end casinos was seen as visually appealing and stood against the competition.
  • • The ‘free-to-play’ mode was appreciated against competitors requiring signup to browse games.
  • • The AmuletoBet name was understood to be Brazil-specific.
  • • Revenue was €20 million after three months.
