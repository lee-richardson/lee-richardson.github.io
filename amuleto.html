Designed a new product for Brazil

Oct 2021 - Apr 2022

AmuletoBet

Methods ↴

Stakeholder interviews, Competitive analysis, Journey mapping, Prototype testing, wireframing, UI design.

Description ↴

I worked with one other designer full-time for six months to create a new brand with the goal of attracting new users.

The challenge ↴

Brazil has a large number of competing brands when it comes to iGaming, and we had to stand out against the competition.

The goal ↴

The business needs were get new users through the signup and deposit flow easily, and differentiate ourselves from our competitors with a Unique Selling Point (USP).

Discover

A product team was created to focus on the brand, working in agile sprints. A product owner was hired in Brazil, to give cultural context and insights. The rest of the team included:

  • • Stakeholders (business analyst, payments and risk)
  • • Developers
  • • Two designers - myself and another product designer, collaborating daily.

Some initial notes from the project kickoff meeting focusing on business requirements.

To launch in line with the team’s backlog, business requirements wanted to redesign another Hero product and brand it ‘AmuletoBet’. This would be a Minimum Viable Product (MVP), keeping the existing product’s user journey. This meant our design thinking was to create a brand identity and redesign the homepage. Our Key Performance Indicator (KPI) focused on a new user signing up and completing a first deposit into their digital wallet.

Define

We had a competitor analysis session to learn more about the market and give us clear evidence for our design decisions. We chose 8 direct competitors and defined parameters, including:

  • • The user journey, focusing on the path to signup and first deposit.
  • • Design patterns.
  • • Visual UI design.
  • • Dark UX patterns.

I took screenshots during competitor analysis to track user flows, focusing on signup.

In terms of UI, we quickly noticed the competition was not localized to Brazil. Instead, sites designed in Asia, Europe and the US offered Brazilian players an identical homepage design with a language selector. This gave us an opportunity for an Unique-Selling-Point (USP) to design the homepage to cater to a Brazilian audience.

During competitor analysis we noticed products shared aesthetic features, which gave us an opportunity to differentiate our product.

In terms of UX, we found competitors required a new user to sign up to access features, and even to see which games were offered. For our user flow, we focused on:

  • • Highlighting our features and USPs on the homepage to boost user’s interest.
  • • Allow games to be played in a ‘free-to-play’ mode, to motivate users to sign up and play for real.
  • • Ease-of-use around the signup flow. Starting the flow should be no more than three clicks away from any screen.
  • • After signup or login, users are directed to the product’s sport betting features, and not casino games. This was to take away focus on the casino aspect of the product.

A rough sketch of landing page ideation, with focus on USPs.

We presented our findings to the product team and used a 4-hour brainstorming workshop to iterate brand directions. We ended with four possible options.

We presented the brand directions to product management.

Early UI ideas for the brand directions helped show our design thinking.

We presented these in a product team meeting, with the casino chip idea being chosen to develop because of its link to casino games and the green-and-yellow design being an USP based on the Brazillian flag. I wanted to confirm the user flow was optimized to improve signup rates. This meant investigating the site’s structure. Over a day, I created a sitemap that gave a view of how the screens are prioritized, related and categorized.

A screenshot of the sitemap, focusing on the initial flow for a new user.

An extended view of the sitemap I created to give an overview of the product.

Develop

I created low-fidelity prototypes of the entire product. We guerilla tested with five employees of Hero Gaming outside of the AmuletoBet product team. The task-completion list was:

  • • Starting from the landing page, complete the signup flow.
  • • Make a first deposit into their digital wallet.

In terms of UI, we began logo ideation. We workshopped on weights, focusing on legibility at small sizes (for the mobile device UI).

Exploration of the logo concept in different weights.

While working on the UI, we realized that the brand around luck direction didn’t fully connect with the brand. Product management were now expecting the product’s sports features would be more popular than the casino games. When we tested the logo on sports-focused material, the design felt out of place. Also the design didn’t stand out when placed against competitors.

Some notes around the shift to sport-focused marketing, requiring a design rethink.

We created this brand direction, but decided to rework our focus because of a disconnect between the UI and sports imagery.

In a meeting with the product team, we decided to scrap this idea and focus on the previously defined idea around luxury. We began more competitor analysis and noticed aesthetics based on game assets and cartoon mascots. To stand out with an elegant and refined design, we researched brands connected with luxury. As our product owner said, “Imagine James Bond in Monaco instead of a weekend in Las Vegas.”

Competitor analysis included brands outside the iGaming space. The centre row shows our logo concept along luxury brands.

Through iteration, we created a dark colour palette that signified luxury and high-end brands. In week-long ideation sessions, the logo developed into a combined ‘A’ and ‘B’ representing AmuletoBet. During guerilla user testing with five Hero employees, this was found to be more readable on the dark UI than the original concept.

Top-left to bottom-right shows the development of the logo to improve legibility on a dark UI.

We tested the brand direction to confirm if it met the business needs of aligning with our sports-betting users. We took stock images of football and beach volleyball, both popular in Brazil, and incorporated the brand to background advertising banners.

We mocked up advertising banners — see background — to test the logo against sports environments.

A mockup of advertising banners - see background - in a football stadium.

In terms of UX, I created wireframes of every screen, and cross-checked them against the site map to confirm the user journey made sense. To speed up my design workflow, I built a design system using components designed by myself and my colleague. Based on the wireframes, I then spent a few days creating high-fidelity prototypes in Figma with the UI applied.

A wireframe of the landing page header, where we experimented in highlighting USPs.

We built high-fidelity prototypes to confirm the user flow met our goals. I created a design system to speed up the process.

To solve the business requirement of getting users to the signup flow quickly, we contained the signup flow in a sliding drawer, which was always accessible from the sidebar menu when logged out. We guerilla tested the core user flow with five Hero Gaming employees who were unfamiliar with the product. Each test confirmed that the signup flow was easy to find and understandable, and was completed with no issues.

Completing the signup flow was a main KPI. Our high-fidelity prototype test confirmed that the signup flow was easily findable.

Deliver

Comfortable that the user flow met our product goals, the UI was checked. We focused on readability and meeting accessibility standards, before presenting to the product team and implementing.

The delivered UI for the landing page header, emphasizing USPs and a different experience to our competitors.

Result

The product was launched in spring 2022. A user interviews session carried out by an agency in Brazil showed overall positive user feedback including:

  • • The ‘AmuletoBet’ name was recognised among users to be Brazil-specific.
  • • The aesthetic of high-end casinos was seen as visually appealing and stood against the competition.
  • • Users were not confused or appeared lost during the main flow of signup and deposit.
