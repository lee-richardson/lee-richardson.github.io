Designed a new product for Brazil

AmuletoBet

Oct 2021 - Apr 2022

Lee

Methods ↴

Stakeholder interviews, Competitive analysis, Journey mapping, Prototype testing, wireframing, UI design.

Description ↴

Hero Gaming was expanding into Latin America with a new brand, focusing on Brazil as the first test market.

The challenge

With no staff in Brazil, and no real insight into how online casinos operate in Brazil, we were struggling to enter the market confidently.

The goal

We wanted to launch a product in the new market and stand out with a Unique Selling Point (USP).

A team was formed to focus on the brand. A product owner was hired in Brazil to give context and insight. The rest of the product team included:

  • • Stakeholders
  • • Developers
  • • Two designers - myself and another product designer, collaborating daily.

In order to launch the product quickly, a decision was made to reskin an existing product. This meant the design team’s role was to create a brand UI, including logo, colours and typography.

The process

Understand

We needed to define a brand direction, and had a kickoff meeting to create some ideas. Initially we were inspired by the Brazilian culture around luck and superstition. We named the site after the amulet that many Brazilians carry daily, which is believed to offer protection from harm, ward off evil spirits, and bring good fortune, and can usually be found worn as jewelry.

Research into the good luck charms called amuletos.

This theme allowed us to include references to amulets and luck into the design, emphasizing that using our site would bring users good luck.

Research

We began studying luck, what it means globally and to Brazilians, and how luck can tie into the casino experience.

At this point we began a competitor analysis, to see if a design based around the cocnept of luck would stand out against the competition. This analysis included a deep dive into UI and flow of other websites, to see if any patterns emerged.

An infographic displaying different symbols of luck around the world.

Our competition analysis included a comparison of logo styles.

We looked at how other casino sites in Brazil set up their landing pages.

Analyze

After presenting our research to the team, we agreed to continue with the idea of a lucky amulet as the logo, and the basis for the UI. In terms of colour schemes, we were thinking a green as a basis. This was referring to the Brazilian flag, and we noticed that it could be a chance to gain a USP, as most our competitors were using dark colour schemes.

Sketch

Moving from paper sketches, we created mockups of logo ideas in Illustrator, and applied iterations based on legibility and how colours could be applied.

An exploration into logo style and how the Brazilian flag colours could be applied.

We designed quick logo mockups based on the concept of luck and mystery.

Wireframes and prototype

We had an existing journey map, but wanted wireframes of every screen to confirm that the flow was optimized, with no confusing parts. Over a few days we created the entire site in wireframes.

To confirm the flow of the site, we created wireframes of every screen to use alongside our journey map.

An early mockup of the landing page.

We tried different designs for our UI and logo and found that the idea of luck wasn't working well enough. We knew that our targer market in Brazil were interested in both casino games and sports betting, and we expected that sports would be a larger user segment than casino games. When we tested our logo with a sporty look, the lucky charm design didn't look like it belonged.

Mockups of advertising banners that shows the conflict between the logo and sports imagery.

Test design and iterate

We decided to scrap this idea, instead focusing on the idea of luxury and high-level casinos. As our product owner said, “Imagine James Bond in Monaco instead of a weekend in Las Vegas.”

Based on our analysis of competitors in the online casino industry, we noticed that many other online casinos used bright and flashy designs, so we aimed to stand out by offering a more elegant and refined look. To achieve this, we looked at luxury brands that used simple and understated designs with muted colors.

Our new logo concept (middle row) took inspiration from high-end brands.

Top left to bottom right shows the development of the logo.

The landing screen was designed to get users playing games fast, and highlight Unique Selling Points (USPs).

To contrast the colourful game tiles, we kept the overall UI subtle and restrained.

We made many changes to the layout, logos, images, and text until we were satisfied. We also tested the logo to make sure it looked good in a sports setting. Once the product team was happy with the updated logo and brand idea, we applied the new design to the wireframes.

We made mockups to see how the logo looked in sports advertisements.

Result

The product was launched in spring 2022, and had overall positive user feedback including:

  • • The AmuletoBet name was recognised as specifically designed for Brazil
  • • The style focusing on luxury was seen as visually appealing.
