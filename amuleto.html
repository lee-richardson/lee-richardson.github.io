Attracting new users with a unique UI

What was the project?

Creating a new brand that increases conversions with an easy-to-find signup flow and strong visuals.

My role

  • — Analysing data analytics.
  • — Competitor analysis.
  • — Creating sitemap and Information Architecture.
  • — Prototype testing.
  • — Wireframes.
  • — UI components.
  • — Creating questions for user feedback session.

Description

I collaborated with another designer to develop a new brand. Our focus was to develop a unique user interface, create an inviting landing page to attract users to sign up, and make sure the signup process was optimised.

The problem

In a crowded market, we needed to stand out from competitors and entice new users to sign up and engage with the product.

The outcome

A new brand with a distinct Unique Selling Point (USP).

Research

In alignment with our team's backlog, business requirements dictated the need to revamp a Hero product and rebrand it as 'AmuletoBet', following a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) approach while retaining the existing product's user journey.


This meant design work was to create a brand identity and redesign the homepage to drive new users into the signup flow. Our Key Performance Indicator (KPI) was centred on new users signing up and successfully completing their initial deposit into their digital wallet.

We were tasked with redesigning this existing Hero product.

To inform our design decisions with solid market insights, I facilitated a competitor analysis session. We selected 8 direct competitors and set evaluation parameters including:

  • — Analysing the user journey, with a focus on the path leading to signup and first deposit.
  • — Examining common design patterns.
  • — Assessing the visual UI design elements.
  • — Identifying any instances of dark UX patterns.

In terms of UI, we quickly noticed the competition was not localised to Brazil. Instead, sites designed in Asia, Europe and the U.S. offered Brazilian players an identical homepage design with a language selector. This realisation presented us with an opportunity for a Unique Selling Point (USP), by tailoring the homepage to cater specifically to a Brazilian audience.

During competitor analysis we focused on signup flows and visual treatments of the products.

Following our competitor analysis, we noticed that most competitors required new users to sign up even just to access basic features and view the available games. In response, we set out our assumptions around user needs with these objectives:

  • — Highlighting our unique features and USPs directly on the homepage to pique user interest without necessitating immediate signup.
  • — Allowing users to play games in a 'free-to-play' mode, and motivating them to sign up and engage with real gameplay.
  • — Allow for an easy signup process, with the signup flow accessible within three clicks from any screen for maximum ease of use.
  • — Upon signup or login, directing users toward the product's sports betting features rather than casino games. Based on our product manager’s market assumptions, users would be more interested in sports than casino games.

Design

We presented our findings to the product team and used a four-hour ideation workshop to iterate brand directions. We ended with four possible options. We presented these in a product team meeting, with the casino chip idea being chosen to develop because of its visual link to casino games.


I wanted to confirm the user flow was optimised to improve signup rates. This meant investigating the site’s structure. Over a day, I created a sitemap that gave a view of how the screens are prioritised, related and categorised.

Prototype

I created low-fidelity prototypes of the entire product. To validate the user experience, we conducted guerilla testing sessions involving five Hero Gaming employees who were not part of the AmuletoBet product team. The task-completion list was:

  • — Starting from the landing page, complete the signup flow.
  • — Make an initial deposit into their digital wallet.

We tested five people with low-fidelity wireframe prototypes.

The overall results of these tests were positive, with no significant issues or concerns raised. However, during our work on the UI, we began to realise that the existing casino chip design didn't align with the brand's identity. Also product management was anticipating that the product's sports-related features would be more popular than the casino games. To address this, we tested the logo on sports-focused material, and the design felt out of place.

We created this brand direction, but decided to rework our focus because of a disconnect between the UI and sports imagery.

Second round of research

In a meeting with the product team, we decided to scrap this idea and shift our focus to a previously defined idea centred around luxury. As we delved deeper into competitor analysis, we noticed that many competitors were relying on aesthetics tied to game assets and cartoon mascots.


To distinguish ourselves with an elegant and refined design, we embarked on research into luxury brands. Our goal was to create an USP that echoed the sentiment articulated by our product owner: "Imagine James Bond in Monaco instead of a weekend in Las Vegas." This became our guiding principle.

Second round of design

Through iteration, we designed a dark colour palette that represented luxury and resonated with high-end brands, while we developed a new logo concept. Guerilla user testing sessions involving five Hero employees confirmed that this new logo concept was more readable and effective on the user interface compared to our original casino-chip idea.

Second round of prototyping

I created wireframes of every screen, and cross-checked them against the site map to confirm the user journey made sense. Based on the wireframes, I then spent a few days creating high-fidelity prototypes in Figma with the UI applied. To solve the business requirement of getting users to the signup flow quickly, we contained the signup flow in a sliding drawer, which was always accessible from the sidebar menu when logged out.


We tested the core user flow with five Hero Gaming employees who were unfamiliar with the product, to represent new users. Each test confirmed that the signup flow was easy to find and understandable, and was completed with no issues.

The high-fidelity prototype showing a games catalogue.

Build

Confident that the user flow aligned with our product objectives, we shifted our attention to the UI. Our focus was on enhancing readability and ensuring compliance with accessibility standards. We presented our work to the product team and collaborated with the developers for implementation.


A user interview session, conducted by an agency in Brazil, yielded largely positive feedback from users, including:

  • — Users displayed clarity and confidence during the core processes of signing up and making a deposit, with no signs of confusion or feeling lost.
  • — The inclusion of a 'free-to-play' mode was well-received, setting us apart from competitors that required signup to browse games.
  • — The AmuletoBet name was understood by users as being specific to Brazil, reinforcing its relevance and local appeal.

Successes

Revenue was €20 million after three months, and data showed no problems with new users completing the signup flow.
